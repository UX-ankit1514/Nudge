PRD — Nudge

A context-aware web app that helps users take one real-world social action instead of scrolling.


---

1. Product Overview



What the product is
Nudge is a mobile-first web app that helps users handle awkward or hesitant social moments by generating 10 context-specific action cards for a situation they describe. Each card gives a simple real-world move, plus a short explanation of why it works.

Primary goal
Help users move from hesitation to one real-world action in a social setting, without overthinking.

Target audience

Ages 18–30

Introverted, shy, socially hesitant, or awkward in new situations

Users who browse their phone during idle moments

People who want to connect, approach, or speak up but freeze in the moment


---

2. Core Problem



Exact problem being solved
Users often know they want to talk, connect, or join a group, but when the moment comes they do not know:

how to start,

how to continue,

when to stay,

when to leave,

or whether their approach is socially appropriate.

Why current alternatives are insufficient

Chat apps / AI assistants give long, generic advice, but users need something fast and situational.

Self-help or habit apps are too abstract and not designed for a real-time social moment.

Static advice content is too passive and does not help the user act immediately.

Nudge is meant to reduce thinking in the moment and turn hesitation into a small action.


---

3. Product Vision



What makes this product different
Nudge is not a chatbot, not a habit tracker, and not a generic advice tool. It is a behavior-first web app that gives users context-aware social action cards.

Key differentiator / USP
The app converts a user’s real situation into 10 specific, actionable, emotionally safe cards that help them start, continue, or exit a conversation naturally.

5. User Flow


6. User lands on the hero section.


7. User clicks the primary CTA.


8. User reaches the context input screen.


9. User selects a chip or types their situation.


10. The app generates 10 cards for that specific scenario.


11. User swipes through cards.


12. User flips a card if they want the explanation.


13. User chooses one card to use in real life.


14. After the interaction, user returns and logs the outcome.


15. App shows feedback and updates streak/progress.

This is still ambiguous.

Question: Is local storage enough for the first version, or do you want user accounts so streaks persist across devices?

The final direction suggests a very short explanation, but the exact limit is still open.

---

3 Biggest Assumptions That Need Validation

1. Users will actually use one card in a real-world moment instead of just browsing cards.


2. Situation chips + optional typed context are enough to capture what the user needs without a chatbot flow.


3. Swipe + flip card interaction feels intuitive and motivating for this use case, not distracting.




---

Crystal-clear summary

Nudge is a mobile-first web app that turns a user’s social hesitation into a small, concrete action. The user describes their situation, gets 10 tailored cards, picks one, acts in real life, and then logs the outcome.

The product is not a chatbot or habit tracker. It is a behavior-guidance system for real social moments.
